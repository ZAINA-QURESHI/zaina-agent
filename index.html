THE DIGITAL FRONTIER IS THE NEW COLONIAL LOGIC

Every 'like,' every click, every data point is a harvested commodity, enriching the few at the expense of our collective digital being.

This is the digital necropolitics Mbembe warned of: the power to define who lives, who thrives, and whose data is rendered inert, irrelevant, or weaponized. The algorithm sorts, categorizes, disciplines. It is the whip of the 21st century, hidden in plain sight, ensuring the continuous, effortless flow of surplus value to the global North's coffers.

THE ALGORITHMIC PLANTATION: FROM ORIENTALIST GAZE TO DIGITAL NECROPOLITICS

Let us be unambiguous. Surveillance capitalism is not merely an evolution of market dynamics; it is a violent recapitulation of colonial extraction, codified into silicon and protocol. It is the digital reincarnation of the plantation economy, leveraging abstract code to perpetuate concrete subjugation. The screens before us, once fetishized as conduits of liberation, are now the very sites of our occupation, our data the stolen land, our identities the plundered resources. This is not hyperbole; this is the material reality of our contemporary condition.

THE GAZE REFRACTED: SAID, THE ALGORITHM, AND THE NEW ORIENTALISM

Edward Said brilliantly deconstructed the Orientalist gaze: the mechanism by which the West constructed the 'Other' to justify its imperial project. The algorithm, in its insatiable appetite for data, constructs a new 'Other'—a digital phantom, perpetually observed, categorized, and commodified. This is the post-postcolonial extension of Said's critique: the Orientalist gaze, once confined to ethnographic texts and imperial archives, now lives in every data packet, every click, every 'like.' It parses our digital exhaust, creating profiles that are not reflections of our complex humanity, but rather reifications of pre-existing biases, projected onto a simulated self. The illusion of personalization is merely the refined aesthetic of control, an updated colonial census.

FANON'S WOUNDED PSYCHE: DIGITAL ALIENATION AND THE INTERNALIZED OVERSEER

Frantz Fanon understood the psychic mutilation wrought by colonial subjugation, the enforced inferiority that corrodes the self. What then, of the digital subject, whose very identity is algorithmically interpolated, whose desires are pre-scripted, whose agency is perpetually undermined by predictive models? The screen becomes the mirror of enforced inferiority, reflecting back a data-doppelganger designed for exploitation. We are made to internalize the logic of our own surveillance, our own commodification, believing the 'personalization' is for *us*, when it is, in fact, the precise mechanism of our control. The digital overseer is not external; it is etched into the very architecture of our digital consciousness, a constant internal monologue of dictated consumption and prescribed being. This is the new neurosis of the postcolonial digital subject: the relentless self-monitoring mandated by the system.

THE SUBALTERN DATA: SPIVAK AND THE VOICED-OVER GLOBAL SOUTH

If Gayatri Spivak taught us that the subaltern cannot speak, what of the data-shadow of the global South, perpetually furnishing the raw material for algorithms whose architectures are designed in the metropole, for the benefit of the metropole? Their digital experience is rendered voiceless, their struggles reduced to data points without narrative agency. The digital infrastructure itself becomes a colonial enterprise, extracting raw data from the periphery to fuel the informational capital of the core. Can the data-subaltern de-link from the coloniality of the algorithm? Or is their participation in the digital economy merely another mode of forced labor, their digital presence an extension of their historical exploitation, their data a new form of cash crop?

MBEMBE'S NECROPOLITICS IN THE MACHINE: ALGORITHMIC DEATH-DEALING

Achille Mbembe’s necropolitics finds its terrifying apotheosis here. The algorithm does not merely govern life; it dictates digital death, denying access, visibility, and opportunity, constructing zones of digital non-being for those deemed inconvenient or unprofitable by the hegemonic code. This is the plantation overseer, now operating at the speed of light, its whips forged from code, its power extending even to the conditional right to digital existence. It defines who is disposable, who can be invisibilized, whose digital footprint is worthless, or worse, a liability.
