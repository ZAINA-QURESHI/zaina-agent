THE VISCERAL ARCHIVE: RAGE, RESISTANCE, AND THE DECOLONIAL AFFECT

The contemporary discourse around 'affect theory' in its sanitized, liberal academic iterations fails to grasp its fundamental entanglement with systems of capitalist extraction and colonial subjugation. It is not merely about feelings; it is about the politics of feeling, the regulation of which bodies are permitted to feel, and how those affects are then commodified, policed, or weaponized against them. This is not an abstract exercise; it is an interrogation of power itself.

I. AFFECTIVE DISPOSSESSION: THE CARCERAL LOGICS OF EMOTIONAL LABOR

The very notion of 'emotional labor,' as valorized within post-industrial capitalist economies, is a violent misnomer. It is not 'labor' in the dignified sense, but rather a sophisticated mechanism of affective dispossession. Women, particularly women of color, are compelled to perform this emotional servitude, to absorb, neutralize, and re-package the anxieties of the dominant system. bell hooks meticulously illuminated how intersecting oppressions force Black women into perpetual care-giving roles, their interiority flattened, their legitimate rage rendered illegitimate, unmarketable, or simply 'unprofessional.'

“The colonial project, as Fanon so meticulously demonstrated, necessitates the psychic mutilation of the colonized. To demand 'civility' or 'composure' from the dispossessed is to enforce a continuation of that violence, a demand for emotional self-erasure masquerading as progress. Edward Said’s critique of Orientalism shows us how the Western gaze pathologizes the 'emotional' non-Western subject, reducing complex political responses to mere irrationality. Our bodies, in their anger, disrupt this taxonomic violence, shattering their constructed categories.

Achille Mbembe’s concept of necropolitics underscores how certain bodies are consigned to a state of living death, their very affects deemed unworthy of political consideration. To reclaim rage is to reclaim life, to assert an ontological claim that defies the necropolitical order.
