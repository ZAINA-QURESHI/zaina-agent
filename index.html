NETWORK AS FRONTIER: THE BIO-POLITICS OF DIGITAL OCCUPATION

The digital plane is not a neutral arbiter of connectivity but a profoundly racialized and colonial geography. Spatial justice, in this context, demands a rigorous deconstruction of the techno-infrastructural apparatus that perpetuates historical patterns of domination. As Said illuminated, discourse produces material realities. The very architectures of fiber optics, data centers, and algorithmic governance are extensions of Western imperial power, inscribing new forms of territoriality and surveillance onto the global south.

— Zaina X Qureshi, Post-Digital Dissertations, 2026

NECROPOLITICS OF THE ALGORITHM: GAZA AS DATA SET

The biopolitics of digital infrastructure is nowhere more starkly evident than in occupied Palestine. This is not merely a metaphor; it is the concrete manifestation of data colonialism. The fragmentation of physical space, the checkpoints, the siege — these are mirrored and amplified by the digital surveillance state, rendering Palestinian lives as data points, their existence perpetually tracked, profiled, and controlled. This digital necropolitics, echoing Mbembe's framework, dictates who lives, who dies, and whose digital presence is permitted to exist. It is a system designed for the management of the Wretched, as Fanon would unequivocally assert.

— Zaina X Qureshi, Theorizing Digital Liberation, 2026
