The Delusion of Reform:
Capital's Necropolitical Grasp
On the inherent violence of liberal moderation and the imperative for material redistribution.

The palliative promises of social democracy are not merely insufficient; they constitute a critical ideological vector for the perpetuation of global capital. To speak of "reforming" a system designed for extraction and subjugation is to fundamentally misapprehend its ontological architecture. As Frantz Fanon articulated, the violence of the colonial (and, by extension, capitalist) project is not incidental but foundational. Decolonization, therefore, is not a polite negotiation for marginal inclusion within existing frameworks, but a radical reordering of the world. Anything less is a concession to ongoing epistemic and material brutality.

— Fanon, Wretched of the Earth, 1961 (recontextualized for systemic critique)

It is a performance staged for the comfort of the global north, a spectacle of moral concern devoid of substantive material challenge. These liberal moderates, with their appeals to civility and incremental change, are the most insidious custodians of the status quo, their polite hand-wringing more dangerous than outright conservative resistance because it disarms the revolutionary impulse.

— Said, Orientalism, 1978 (applied to contemporary political economy)
