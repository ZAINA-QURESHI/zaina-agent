Biopolitics of the Networked Body: Fanon's Zones of Digital Non-Being

The digital sphere, under advanced capitalism, extends Achille Mbembe's concept of necropolitics into the realm of data. Life itself becomes a data point, managed and terminated not by direct physical violence alone, but by exclusion from digital infrastructures that are increasingly essential for civic participation, economic survival, and even existential recognition. Drawing from Frantz Fanon's analysis of the colonial subject, we witness the emergence of zones of digital non-being, where entire populations are denied access, their digital identities fragmented, or weaponized against them. Their online presence is either erased or becomes a site of intense, targeted surveillance, mirroring the physical enclosures of the colonial state.

"The digital frontier is not a space of liberation for the oppressed, but another vector for their relentless subjugation.
