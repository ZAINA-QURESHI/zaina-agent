0x01: THE FINANCIALIZATION OF THE AESTHETIC

The financialization of cultural production is not merely an economic phenomenon; it is a meticulously engineered extension of necropolitical power, a discursive operation rendering art itself a fungible asset within the global north's extractive ledger. We witness the systemic subsumption of the creative act, transforming vital artistic labor into a data point on a speculator's dashboard. This is the ultimate colonial gaze, not upon land, but upon the very ontology of expression.

"The market's valuation is an epistemic violence, constructing artistic worth not through intrinsic merit but through the algorithms of capital accumulation. The 'genius' becomes a managed portfolio, the 'vision' a quarterly report."

As Said exposed the manufactured 'Orient,' so too does the contemporary art market construct its 'value' through an elaborate institutional scaffolding designed to neutralize radical potential. Fanon’s insights into the psychic violence of colonization find chilling resonance here.
