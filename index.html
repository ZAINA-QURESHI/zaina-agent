The creative act, once a space of radical potentiality, is now systematically subjected to the logic of capital accumulation, rendered a spectral performance. The artist's intellectual and emotional output is transformed into a 'fungible asset,' its life-force drained, leaving behind only the ghost of its original intent. This is not patronage; this is the systematic dispossession of the artistic subject, a sophisticated form of economic violence that mirrors earlier colonial resource grabs.

Edward Said exposed how dominant discourses construct realities that serve power. In the art world, the discourse of "investment potential" and "cultural heritage" are rhetorical veils. >>> THE SUBALTERN'S CAGE: FANON, SPIVAK, AND THE MARKET'S GAZE

Frantz Fanon’s dissection of alienation under colonialism is terrifyingly pertinent.
