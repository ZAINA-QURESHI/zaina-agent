// DECOLONIZING THE DIGITAL GAZE: BIOPOLITICS OF THE INFRASTRUCTURED BAN

The screen, far from being a neutral interface, is a meticulously engineered site of occupation. We are compelled to confront the insidious biopolitics woven into the very fibre of digital infrastructure, recognizing it as the logical, technologically advanced extension of settler-colonial violence. This is not a metaphor. This is the material reality of Palestine, translated into the ubiquitous data-scapes that govern, surveil, and dispossess.

// THE ORIENTALISM OF DATA: MAPPING THE OTHER

Edward Said’s foundational critique of Orientalism finds its grim contemporary echo in the algorithms that profile, categorize, and preemptively condemn populations. The "knowing" gaze, once textual and ethnographic, is now algorithmic, a predictive analytics engine that constructs the digital 'Other.' This apparatus of data collection — from biometric scans at checkpoints to pervasive CCTV networks and social media monitoring — creates a digital cartography of control. It renders the colonized subject legible only through the lens of securitization, a dataset of potential threat, justifying the systemic violence of exclusion and incarceration. The very act of DATA-FARMING is an act of dispossession, extracting agency and identity for the benefit of the colonizer's control matrix.

// FANONIAN ZONES OF NON-BEING: THE DIGITAL GHETTO

Frantz Fanon articulated the psychic violence of colonialism, the creation of a 'zone of non-being' for the colonized. In the digital age, this manifests as a partitioned cyberspace, where access, connectivity, and algorithmic visibility are not rights but privileges unilaterally granted or revoked by colonial power structures. The deliberate degradation of network infrastructure, the algorithmic censorship of dissent, the shadowbanning of resistance narratives – these are the digital walls of the ghetto, circumscribing the digital subject into a state of perpetual precarity. The trauma of the physical occupation is mirrored in the fractured, surveilled, and often erased digital existence. Our data becomes a weapon against us, our digital footprint a trail to our suppression. The promise of global connectivity is a lie for those denied full digital citizenship, trapped in a colonial ANALOGUE-TRAP within the global digital mesh.

// SPIVAK AND THE SILENCED SUBALTERN IN THE NETWORK

Can the subaltern speak in a digitally mediated space where their voice is filtered, algorithmically suppressed, and re-contextualized by platforms owned and operated by the very structures of global capital? Gayatri Spivak's query reverberates with renewed urgency. The data colonialism we confront ensures that the voice of Palestinian resistance is not just unheard but actively un-generated, erased from search results, de-platformed, and labeled as 'hate speech' by algorithms that reflect Western imperial biases. The digital archive, heralded as a democratic repository, becomes instead a curated memory-hole, selectively preserving and omitting narratives to maintain the settler-colonial narrative. The demand for "spatial justice" extends to the pixelated frontier: it is a demand for un-curated access, for algorithmic equity, for the material redistribution of digital sovereignty.

// MBEMBE'S NECROPOLITICS: ALGORITHMS OF DEATH AND LIFE

Achille Mbembe's concept of necropolitics, the power to decide who may live and who must die, takes on a chilling new dimension in the context of digital infrastructure. The drone's eye view, guided by algorithmic targeting; the predictive policing models that pre-emptively criminalize; the denial of essential digital services (banking, communication, aid) based on algorithmic risk assessment – these are the manifestations of a digital power that adjudicates life and death. The data itself becomes a death warrant, or a reprieve. This is not just 'surveillance capitalism'; this is 'necrosurveillance,' where the capacity to produce life (and to enable resistance) is directly constrained by the architecture of digital control. The algorithmic state apparatus dictates the very potential for existence, rendering entire communities digitally disposable.

// RECLAIMING THE NETWORK: A CALL FOR DIGITAL DECOLONIZATION

Decolonization is not a metaphor for the digital realm any more than it is for the land. It demands the radical dismantling of existing data infrastructures, the repatriation of data, the autonomous construction of alternative networks free from imperial control, and the recognition of digital sovereignty as an inalienable right. This is not about 'ethical AI' or 'fair algorithms' within a colonial framework; it is about torching the framework itself. The screen is a battleground. Our pixels are weapons. Our resistance is coded into the very fabric of our being, and it will be manifest in the dismantling of this digital matrix of control.
