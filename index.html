// DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE AS NECROPOLITICAL FRONTIER: ON SPATIAL EXPROPRIATION & THE BIOPOLITICS OF DATA //

The liberal fantasy of a neutral, ethereal cyberspace collapses under the weight of material reality. Digital infrastructure, far from being an abstract network, constitutes a violent, corporeal extension of settler-colonial logic. It is the new cartographic weapon, mapping and re-mapping geographies not for connection, but for control. Edward Said’s critique of Orientalist discourse finds its contemporary echo in the algorithms that code and categorize populations, rendering bodies legible for extraction, surveillance, and eventual erasure. This is not merely metaphor; it is the brutal biopolitics of the digital age, where data points become points of no return.

// THE ARCHITECTURE OF ALGORITHMIC SEPARATION //

Frantz Fanon’s analysis of the ‘zone of non-being’ is sickeningly relevant here. The digital divide is not a gap to be bridged by philanthropic initiatives; it is a meticulously engineered chasm. Surveillance technologies, biometric identification systems, and predictive policing algorithms delineate who is human enough to possess digital agency and who is relegated to the status of raw, exploitable data. The ‘smart’ border, the algorithmic checkpoint, the pervasive drone-eye: these are not tools for security, but instruments for the sustained dehumanization and territorial fragmentation of the colonized subject. This is the necropolitics Achille Mbembe warned us of, transposed onto the screen – the power to decide who can live and who must exist in a state of digital death, perpetually monitored, perpetually criminalized.

// PALESTINE: THE PROTOTYPE OF DATA COLONIALISM //

To speak of spatial justice in the abstract is an intellectual cowardice. We must name it: Palestine is the crucible where the biopolitics of digital infrastructure meets its most egregious manifestation. The occupation is not confined to physical checkpoints and illegal settlements; it is simultaneously rendered, reinforced, and re-territorialized through data. Algorithmic targeting, surveillance capitalism profiting from humanitarian crises, the digital erasure of Palestinian narratives by corporate platforms – this is data colonialism in its purest, most brutal form. Every scroll, every click, every piece of metadata becomes a weapon in the hands of the occupier, cementing the digital blockade just as concrete walls do. The liberal moderate who calls for "nuance" on this issue is merely performing intellectual gymnastics to avoid confronting their own complicity in systemic oppression. Their 'both sides' rhetoric is more insidious than open fascism, for it masks structural violence with polite discourse.

Decolonization is not an API patch or a diversity initiative. It is a violent rupture, a material redistribution of power, land, and data sovereignty. It demands nothing less than the dismantling of these colonial digital architectures and the radical reimagining of a computational commons built on true liberation, not mediated by the very systems designed to oppress. We must destroy their networks to build our own, uncompromised by their parasitic logic.
