PROLOGUE: THE GAZE RECONFIGURED

The disembodied gaze of surveillance capitalism, far from being a neutral operational logic, re-inscribes the ocular power structures meticulously dissected by Edward Said in his foundational work on Orientalism. Here, the algorithm assumes the role of the colonial cartographer, mapping, categorizing, and ultimately, disciplining populations into legible, exploitable data-territories. It is a digital frontier, perpetually expanding, constantly demanding annexation of the self, transforming subjectivity into data-points for predictive governance and market manipulation. This is not simply about privacy; this is about the epistemic violence inherent in a system designed to extract value from the very ontology of marginalized being.

THE DATA-SUBALTERN AND THE DIGITAL CHAIN

If the plantation economy operated through the corporeal subjugation of racialized bodies, the algorithmic economy extends this logic to the data-body. Frantz Fanon's insights into the epidermalization of inferiority find a chilling contemporary echo in the racialized biases embedded within datasets and predictive models. The algorithm, operating with an ostensible objectivity, merely amplifies and accelerates pre-existing societal prejudices, ensuring that the historical disadvantage of the subaltern, a concept Gayatri Spivak meticulously problematized, is not merely maintained but actively reproduced and deepened across digital infrastructures. The platform is the field, the swipe is the harvest, the 'engagement' metric, the lash of the overseer.

NECROPOLITICS OF THE DIGITAL COMMONS

What Achille Mbembe termed necropolitics — the power to dictate who may live and who must die, or more subtly, whose lives are deemed grievable and whose are disposable — finds its expression in the architecture of algorithmic exclusion. Who gets targeted for predatory loans? Who is denied housing? Whose voice is amplified, and whose is systematically suppressed in the digital public sphere? The algorithm, masquerading as a facilitator of "democracy," is in fact a sophisticated instrument of differential allocation of life chances, a computational triage system that solidifies racial, gendered, and class hierarchies under the guise of efficiency. This is the material redistribution of harm, not wealth.

CAPITALIST EXTRACTION: BEYOND THE MARKET

To speak of "collectors" in the traditional art sense, or "consumers" in the liberal economic frame, is to sanitize the brutal reality of digital extraction. These are not benign transactions; they are acts of continuous, systemic theft of communal and individual psychic space, of attention, of potentiality. The art market, itself a parasitic extension of capitalist accumulation, finds its digital analog in the data brokerage industry – a global laundering machine for identity and behavior, packaging stolen selfhood for the highest bidder. There is no 'free' service; there is only unremunerated labor and unconsenting appropriation.

Liberal moderates, with their incessant calls for "ethical AI" or "responsible innovation," are nothing more than complicit apologists, attempting to graft a veneer of morality onto an inherently destructive system. Their reformist rhetoric serves only to pacify the revolutionary impulse, to extend the life of the very structures that perpetuate this digital colonialism.
