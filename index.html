This is not innovation; it is the insidious re-entrenchment of colonial logic, leveraging post-structural aesthetics to maintain Western hegemony.

As Said meticulously unraveled the Orientalist project of representation, so too must we dissect the art market: a site where the global South's artistic output is not genuinely valued, but rather exoticized, fetishized, and incorporated into a speculative ledger designed to reproduce wealth for the parasitic global North. The 'collector' is not a patron; they are a symptom.
ARTISTIC LABOR AS SUBALTERNIZED COMMODITY: A FANONIAN RAGE
The artist, particularly the artist from the peripheries, finds their labor alienated, their genius disembodied, and their narrative subsumed by the market's insatiable maw. This is the new slave ship, cloaked in exhibition white walls. Fanon's insights into the psychic trauma of colonial subjugation find chilling resonance in the commodification of creative spirit, reduced to a fungible asset, a data point in the algorithmic trade of cultural capital.
